the otos
Otocinclus sp. — Dwarf Suckermouth Catfish
about
there are five of them, and they are constantly working. otocinclus are tireless algae grazers — they patrol every leaf, glass panel, and hardscape surface with their little sucker mouths. they feel most secure in groups, which tracks because they're almost always clustered together. one of their most endearing quirks: when resting on a surface, their little pectoral fins lift their bodies up so they appear to hover just above it, like tiny tripod fishers just hanging out. supplement their diet with blanched zucchini when natural algae runs low.

the amanos
Caridina multidentata — Amano Shrimp
about
named after aquascaping legend Takashi Amano, who popularized their use in planted tanks in the 1980s. i bought 11, but they're nearly translucent and constantly moving so it's genuinely hard to do a headcount — they could all be there, who's to say. they forage on algae, biofilm, and everyone's leftovers without needing any help. you'll forget they exist and then suddenly spot five of them on one leaf doing an extremely thorough job.
