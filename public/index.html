THE TANK TEAM
fish friend
Colomesus asellus — South American Puffer

Fish friend was a snail control employee at Aqua Forest Aquarium. we met in august 2025 and he looked at me with his big eyes, smol face and zoomed around super fast. I visited him every week for 6 months until the store finally let me have him. This tank was made to be his perfect retirement home.

No matter where he's hiding in the tank, he's always happy to swim up and say "o hi!" whenever i walk by.

amazon puffer gang
Colomesus asellus — South American Puffer

Recruited specifically so fish friend would have a crew to shoal with. meet lil smol, lil brave, lil shy, and lil dumpy. they're smaller and definitely not as smart or as good at hunting snails as fish friend, but fish friend has been happy to show them the ropes. fish friend has never been alone since.

the otos
Otocinclus sp. — Dwarf Suckermouth Catfish

Otocinclus are algae grazers that lick every surface with their little sucker mouths. They feel most secure in groups so you can usually see the six of them grazing together.

When resting on a surface, they lower their dorsal fin like a sail and sit on their little pectoral fins, like tiny tripods.

I supplement their diet with blanched zucchini when natural algae runs low. It's like crack for them.

the amanos
Caridina multidentata — Amano Shrimp

Named after aquascaping legend Takashi Amano, who popularized their use in planted tanks in the 1980s.

I bought 11, but they're nearly translucent and constantly moving so it's genuinely hard to do a headcount. They could all be there, or they could've gone to the big tank in the sky, who's to say.

They forage on algae, biofilm, and everyone's leftovers so I never have to worry about feeding them. I'll forget they exist and then suddenly spot five of them on one leaf doing an extremely thorough job.

Tank: 75cm long × 45cm deep × 45cm high (roughly 40 gallons).

Light: Twinstar Aquarium Light (C Series) on a UNS Bar Pro Light Hanging Kit

Filter: Fluval 307 with glass Lily input/output pipes

Tank lid: There were literally zero glass lids that could fit this size of aquarium so I made my own by going to the local hardware shop and got glass cut to size.

Tank stand: It's a heavy duty black steel frame that I was able to get secondhand with the tank! The guy I bought it from said he custom made it himself so kudos to his stellar work.
